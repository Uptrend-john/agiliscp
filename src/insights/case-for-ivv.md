Organizations invest millions of dollars in strategic initiatives — ERP implementations, technology platform migrations, business process redesigns. Yet studies consistently show that a significant percentage of these projects fail to deliver their intended value. Many run over budget, miss deadlines, or simply don't achieve the business outcomes they were designed to produce.

One of the most effective tools for protecting these investments is one that many organizations overlook: **Independent Validation & Verification (IV&V)**.

## What Is IV&V?

Independent Validation & Verification is the practice of engaging a qualified third party — completely independent of the project team, system integrator, or software vendor — to objectively assess the health, risk, and trajectory of a strategic initiative.

Think of it as a project health check performed by someone who has no stake in the project's outcome other than its genuine success.

## Why Independence Matters

The key word in IV&V is *independent*. When the team delivering a project is also responsible for reporting on its health, there are natural conflicts of interest:

- **Vendor optimism**: System integrators may underreport risks to protect their relationship and contract.
- **Internal bias**: Project managers may unconsciously minimize issues to avoid difficult conversations with sponsors.
- **Scope creep blindness**: Teams embedded in the daily work can lose sight of the bigger picture.

An independent advisor has the freedom — and the obligation — to tell you what you need to hear, not what you want to hear.

## When Should You Consider IV&V?

IV&V is most valuable in scenarios where:

- **The stakes are high**: Mission-critical systems, significant capital investment, or major operational impact
- **The project is complex**: Multiple vendors, cross-functional dependencies, or new-to-the-organization technology
- **You've been surprised before**: If previous projects delivered unexpected results, IV&V builds the early warning system you were missing
- **External stakeholders need assurance**: Board members, investors, or regulators need confidence in project progress

## What Does IV&V Actually Deliver?

A rigorous IV&V engagement typically includes:

1. **Project health assessment** — objective evaluation of scope, timeline, budget, and quality
2. **Risk identification** — proactive surfacing of issues before they become crises
3. **Vendor oversight** — independent review of vendor deliverables and performance
4. **Governance recommendations** — strengthening decision-making frameworks and escalation paths
5. **Executive reporting** — clear, actionable dashboards for leadership

## The ROI of IV&V

The cost of IV&V is typically a small fraction of the overall project budget — usually 3-5%. But the value it protects can be enormous. Catching a critical issue early can save weeks of rework, prevent costly scope changes, or even save the entire initiative from failure.

As the old saying goes: the best time to fix a problem is before it becomes one.
